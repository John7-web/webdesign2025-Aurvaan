Top colours used on webpages (and why)

  1. Blue - trust, links, and primary CTAs. Common because users expect blue to be clickable and it reads well on white backgrounds.
  2. White / Off-white - backgrounds and negative space; maximizes readability and makes other colours pop.
  3. Black / Dark Gray - primary text for legibility.
  4. Gray neutrals - UI chrome, borders, and secondary text to create hierarchy without competing with main content.
  5. Red / Orange / Yellow - alerts, errors, and attention. Red for destructive actions or errors; orange/yellow for warnings/promotions.
  6. Green / Teal - success states and positive actions; associated with safety and 'go'.
  7. Purple / Pink - accents for creative or premium brands; used sparingly for emphasis.
  8. Gradients & multi-colour accents - used in hero areas and branding for depth and modern look; ensure text contrast still works.

Color harmonies

Color harmonies are ways of picking colours that look balanced together.

Complementary

Two colours opposite each other on the colour wheel.

#FF0000
#00FFFF






Monochromatic

Different tints and shades of a single hue.

#FF7F00 (dark)
#FF9F41 (mid)
#FFCB99 (light)

Analogous

Colours next to each other on the wheel.

#0000FF
#0080FF
#00FFFF

Triadic

Three colours evenly spaced on the wheel.

#FF0000
#0000FF
#00FF00

Tetradic (double complementary)

Four colours forming two complementary pairs.

#FF0000
#8000FF
#80FF00
#00FFFF

Warm vs Cool colours

Colours are often described as warm or cool. Warm colours feel energetic, cozy, and attention-grabbing. Cool colours feel calm, trustworthy, and restful.

Shades, Tints and Tones

These are ways to change a base hue:

  • Tint - add white to make a lighter version.
  • Shade - add black to make a darker version.
  • Tone - add gray (white+black) to reduce saturation.

Hue, Saturation & Luminance (HSL)

HSL is a way to describe colour:

  • Hue - the position on the colour wheel
  • Saturation - how pure or vivid the colour is
  • Luminance - how light or dark the colour is
