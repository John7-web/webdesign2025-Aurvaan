General Facts About Whales

Classification

All whales belong to the order Cetacea and are divided into two suborders: Mysticetes (baleen whales, which filter feed) and Odontocetes (toothed whales, which are active predators and use echolocation).

The Largest Mammal

The Blue Whale is the largest animal ever known to have lived on Earth, exceeding even the largest dinosaurs. Due to their size, their bodies are perfectly supported by the buoyancy of seawater.

Ecosystem Role

Whales are vital for ocean health. Their vertical migration and nutrient-rich feces (known as the "whale pump") help circulate nutrients from the deep sea to the surface, feeding phytoplankton, which in turn produce oxygen.
